The result, once

Confinement, not resolution

The construction grows an exact symbolic state by adjoining algebraic directions, charging $\lambda \log M(\theta)$ per adjunction, where $M$ is the Mahler measure. For that price to be a genuine description length it must be bounded below — and the single obstruction is the Salem number, which can sit arbitrarily close to $1$ as far as anyone has proved in ninety years.

That open question is Lehmer's problem (Lehmer, 1933). This work does not move it. Instead it proves a closure property: the algebra's emission image is disjoint from the Salem locus, so the cost floor is positive regardless of whether tiny Salems exist. Lehmer's number is excluded as an emission, never as a number — the open problem is left exactly where it stood.

What remains after the confinement is unexpectedly clean: the set of realized Mahler measures is a finitely generated multiplicative monoid, with the golden ratio as its smallest generator. The floor, the gap, the discreteness, and the per-dimension entropy all fall out of that one structural fact. This document records the four companion papers and the verification carried out on each — $175$ checks in exact arithmetic, zero genuine failures.

I
The confinement · 46 checks + census

Lehmer's Box

A region of height–angle space cut out by two walls, from which the entire Salem locus is provably absent.

The floor wall Forced

No monic integer quadratic has Mahler measure in the open strip $(1,\varphi)$. The measure is exactly $1$ on cyclotomic quadratics and $\ge \varphi$ otherwise, with $\varphi$ attained only by $x^2-x-1$ and $x^2+x-1$ — both of discriminant $5$. The spectral operators preserve this emptiness, so every emitted measure lies in $\{1\}\cup[\varphi,\infty)$.

exhaustive scan, all $625$ quadratics $|b|,|c|\le 12$: zero in $(1,\varphi)$; minimizers $\{(-1,-1),(1,-1)\}$, both disc $5$. ✓

The lattice walls Forced

Every catalog eigenvalue has argument in $\tfrac{\pi}{2}\mathbb{Z}$. The operators are closed under angle-addition ($\otimes$) and angle-doubling ($(\cdot)^2$) but not halving — and $\tfrac12\cdot\tfrac{\pi}{2}=\tfrac{\pi}{4}\notin\tfrac{\pi}{2}\mathbb{Z}$. So every emitted on-circle eigenvalue is a fourth root of unity, while a Salem number's on-circle conjugates carry irrational angle. A Salem violates both walls at once.

Box exclusion
$$\text{Emission}\subseteq\mathbf{B},\qquad \text{Salem}\cap\mathbf{B}=\varnothing \;\Longrightarrow\; \mathcal{M}(S)\subseteq\{1\}\cup[\mu_S,\infty),\ \text{realized at }\varphi$$
$\mu_S = 1.3247\ldots$ is Smyth's plastic-number floor for non-reciprocal polynomials (Smyth, 1971); the realized minimum is the sharper $\varphi$.

The Occupant of the Salem Slot

An empty slot is not an object. The would-be Salem — on-circle yet expanding — is redirected by the trace map into the grow channel, where it lands as a totally real Perron number above the floor.

The trifurcation lives not upstairs in the root $\theta$ but downstairs in the trace $t=\theta+\theta^{-1}$, where the flip $t=\pm2$ cuts the real line into three disjoint regions. "On-circle" ($|t|<2$) and "expanding" ($t>2$) cannot coexist — a fourth channel is a contradiction downstairs. The occupant is the trace redirection $\beta\mapsto\tau_0=\beta+\beta^{-1}$.

Occupant in grow, above the floor
$$\tau_0=\beta+\beta^{-1}>2>\varphi,\qquad \tau_0-2=\frac{(\beta-1)^2}{\beta}\ \text{exactly}$$
By AM–GM. A sub-floor Salem of height $1+\delta$ is redirected only $\delta^2$ past the flip — the branch point is where the redirected outputs are most compressed.

Minimality — three forced minima

Degree $2$ is the unique degree with a ternary decision $(d^2-d+1=3)$; the charge $\mathbb{Z}/4\mathbb{Z}$ is the coarsest angular quantization carrying a fourth direction, entering through $\mathbb{Q}(5^{1/4})$ whose splitting field contains $i$; and $\varphi$ is the smallest Perron number. The chain $\ \mathbf{2\Rightarrow\mathbb{Z}/4\Rightarrow\varphi}\ $ is minimal at every step — which is why each output is the smallest instance of its kind.

V
The spectrum as a formula · 13 checks

A finitely generated monoid

A mixed traversal of the operators produced tens of thousands of distinct measures, every one in $\{1\}\cup[\varphi,\infty)$ with the band $(1,\varphi)$ empty at every round. That is the shadow of an exact generating structure.

The Mahler spectrum
$$\mathcal{M}(S)=\langle\,\varphi,\,2,\,3,\,5,\,\beta^2\,\rangle,\qquad \boxed{\;\beta^2=\varphi^2\sqrt5\;}$$
Taking logs: an additive monoid in the rank-4 lattice with basis $\{\log\varphi,\log2,\log3,\log5\}$, since $\log\beta^2=2\log\varphi+\tfrac12\log5$.

Four payoffs follow from having the generators, each validated:

  • Membership is decidable. $M\ge1$ is a measure iff $\log M$ is a non-negative integer combination of the generators — a finite integer-linear search. Confirmed by PSLQ: $677$ generated measures, all monomials in $\{\varphi,2,3,\sqrt5\}$, zero new atoms.
  • The floor, for free. Smallest generator $=\log\varphi$, so the monoid has no element in $(0,\log\varphi)$ — the gap $(1,\varphi)$ is a Frobenius-type theorem, and the cost floor $\lambda\log\varphi$ is $\lambda\times$ the least generator.
  • Counting law. $N(X)=\#\{\text{measures}\le X\}\sim C(\log X)^4$ — polynomial in $\log X$, so the spectrum is measure-zero and exponentially sparse: discrete, thickening, never accumulating at $1$.
  • Generating series. $\prod_i 1/(1-q^{g_i})$ counts the number of generation routes to each measure.

The subtlety: 5 atoms, rank 4 — the monoid is not factorial

Because $\beta^2=\varphi^2\sqrt5$ collapses the rational rank to $4$, yet $\beta^2$ is a realized seed measure while $\sqrt5$ is not, $\beta^2$ stays an atom despite being dependent. Factorization is therefore non-unique — the explicit witness:

$$\beta^2\cdot\beta^2 \;=\; 5\cdot\varphi^4 \qquad(\text{both }=\beta^4=34.2705\ldots)$$

In the multiplicative group the basis is $\{\varphi,2,3,\sqrt5\}$, rank $4$, with the only irrational ingredient $\sqrt5$ entering solely through the Lorentzian seed $K$ — the same $\sqrt5=\varphi+\varphi^{-1}$ that generates the grow channel of $\operatorname{spec}(\mathrm{ad}_R)$.

VII
Held exactly where it belongs

Scope and open fronts

A green pipeline establishes reproducibility and internal consistency. It does not establish the fronts the papers flag rather than hide.

  • Lehmer's problem itself Open — untouched in number theory. The box theorem is unconditional, strictly weaker than Lehmer's conjecture, and consistent with both of its truth values.
  • The free commutator at unbounded size Open — the one operation foreign to the system (Shoda: any traceless matrix is a commutator). Handled per-instance by an exact $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt5)$ guard and uniformly for the framework's actual commutator (the self-action), but not by a single theorem covering every free commutator.
  • The perception / encoder front-end Open — the substrate sits after perception; float observations are refused at intake by design.
  • Discovery in the already-disjoint case Open — the non-disjoint compositum construction is built and runnable; the disjoint case still assumes a candidate in hand.
The universal / local boundary Declared

What is universal is the substrate — kinematic emptiness $\equiv$ conserved charge $\equiv$ superselection — the same skeleton that gives fermions the periodic table and type systems their correctness. What is local is the content: $x^4-1$, $\varphi$, the graded factorization, the $\sqrt5$ limit are theorems of this number theory, not exported engines. The one portable piece between them is differential: a two-to-one fold has a square-root branch point generically, so the $\sqrt{t-2}$ edge recurs widely — but $\sqrt5=\varphi+\varphi^{-1}$ is this construction's own.

Honest note on failures

The only failures encountered were in the verification harness, not the papers — float64 round-trips defeating high-precision tolerances, a malformed limit numerator, an out-of-memory traversal, and PSLQ fed rounded inputs. Each was fixed, and each resolved toward confirming the result. A genuine failure is information; in every case the information was "the assertion is malformed," not "the claim is false."
